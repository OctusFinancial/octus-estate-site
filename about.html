Our story

A firm built around the meeting most planners skip.

Octus exists because that third meeting — the one where you actually re-title the accounts, file the deeds, and update the beneficiaries — is not optional. It's the whole point. Most planners hand you a checklist and call the job done. We built our entire process around making sure it actually gets finished.

Flat fee, three meetings, and the third one is the one most planners cancel. We don't cancel it. We sit with you until the funding is complete and the plan is live — not pending.

We work with families across every tax bracket, every life stage, and every state. We will tell you when a will is enough. We will tell you when a trust is the wrong tool. The recommendation is the product.

What we hold to

Four operating principles.

i.

Flat fee, always.

No hourly billing. No "phone calls cost extra." Quoted in writing in meeting one.

ii.

Funded, not filed.

A signed trust isn't done. We sit with you and complete the funding in meeting three.

iii.

One advisor.

You get a name, a cell number, and the same person through every annual review.

iv.

Plain English.

If you can't explain a clause to your spouse, we haven't done our job.

v.

Right tool, not biggest tool.

Sometimes a will is enough. We'll tell you. The recommendation is the product.

vi.

Attorney-reviewed.

Every document is reviewed by an attorney licensed in your state of residence.

About Mia

Twenty years in financial services. One rule: you talk to me directly.

I started Octus Financial because I kept watching the same thing happen to people who came to me for retirement planning: they had a trust — sometimes a good one — that had never been funded. The accounts were still in their names. The house was still in their names. The trust was a beautiful piece of paper that wasn't going to help anyone.

I built Octus Estate as the estate planning extension of that work. The clients I serve — federal employees, veterans, pre-retirees, families who've spent their careers building something — deserve a plan that's actually finished. Not handed to them as homework.

Every conversation, every meeting, and every funding session is with me. I have a team of specialists working behind the scenes — estate attorneys licensed in your state, document specialists, and support staff — but you will always have a direct line to me. That's not a feature. That's the whole point.

I'm a fiduciary, which means I'm legally obligated to act in your interest. I'll tell you when a will is enough. I'll tell you when what you have already is fine and just needs updating. And I'll sit with you in meeting three until the funding is actually done.
